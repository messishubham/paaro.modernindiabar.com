About Paro

A 1940s mansion.
A modern
Indian bar.

Paro occupies the first and second floors of Jaipuria Mansion — a pre-Independence building on Panch Batti, MI Road. Indigenous lime-plastered walls. A bar island built for conversation. Chandeliers that move with the evening breeze.

The name is Rajasthani — romantic, devoted, unforgettable. The concept is modern: cocktails built from kokum, butterfly-pea, rose, and fine whisky; food that reclaims the genius of Indian street cooking and puts it on your terms.

From sold-out cocktail takeovers in Delhi and Mumbai to a feature in Condé Nast Traveller — Paro has moved from a Jaipur address to a national conversation. The building is eighty years old. The evening is always new.

The Bar

The cocktail menu
starts with India.
Ends with tonight.

Every spirit is chosen. Every infusion is house-made. Kokum from the Konkan coast. Butterfly-pea from the northeast. Rose from Rajasthan. The menu is built around the subcontinent — not around trends. What you get in your glass has a reason to be there.

The Bar

Craft Cocktails · Indian Spirits

Butterfly-Pea Gin · Tonic · Rosemary

Saanjh

Shifts from deep violet to soft blush as the evening deepens. Butterfly-pea gin and tonic, served with a smoking rosemary sprig. Jaipur at dusk — in a glass.

House SignatureBest Seller

Rose Gin · Elderflower · Tonic

Shehar

Named for the city, coloured like its walls. Rose-forward gin with elderflower, served in a crystal coupe with a toasted marshmallow. Delicate, fragrant, unmistakably pink.

House Signature

Whisky · Kokum Shrub · Cumin Salt

Ratnagiri Rhapsody

The Konkan coast meets the Thar desert. Kokum's natural tartness against cumin-spiced whisky, served short over a single ice block. The one people reorder.

House Signature

Whisky · Garam Masala Syrup · Ginger

Garam Masala

Warming whisky, house-made garam masala syrup, ginger, and a cinnamon stick. Exactly what the name promises — only better than expected. The one that converts whisky sceptics.

House Signature

Butter-Washed Rum · Cream Froth · Popcorn

Makkhan Madira

Fat-washed rum, whipped cream froth, salted popcorn on top. Served in a hand-engraved brass cup. Indulgent, playful, impossible to finish without smiling.

House FavouriteMust Try

Whisky · Chilli Tincture · Mysore Pak

Spicy Mysore

A South Indian sweet meets a North Indian winter spirit. House chilli tincture in whisky, served with a Mysore Pak garnish. Sweet heat. Absolutely no apology.

The Kitchen

Indian cooking,

what makes it good.

The menu is organised the way India actually eats — by mood, not by course. Chaat to start. Kebabs to share. A slow curry if the evening calls for it. Every dish has a reason to be on the table. Nothing is there for decoration.
